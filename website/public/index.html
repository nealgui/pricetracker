Our Vision

What is the Purpose of this Project?
Our goal is to provide users with the ability to compare products across various websites using different metrics, where price comparison is the most important aspect. Users will be able to search for specific items by name, and retrieve information such as price, description, delivery details, and images of items. Effectively, we want to provide an application where users can obtain a clear view of the ways in which a product can differ when it is available on numerous websites.

Who are the Users?
The primary target audience includes customers and sellers. We aim to make the application simple and intuitive for the average shopper looking to save money on online shopping, while also providing further analytics on products (such as price history) to appeal to distributors looking to collect market data to help with their research.

Why is this Project Valuable?
Customers can reduce their overall spending costs by simply looking up a single product and finding the retailer that offers the lowest price for that product. This will allow them to save money without having to perform all of the necessary work that comes with online price comparison, and may encourage them to be more frugal in the future. For sellers, the value can be found in the product analytics such that they can collect market data for products that are similar to their own and remain competitive when it comes to pricing strategies.

Velocity

Estimate vs. Actual

It was very challenging accurately assessing our group's velocity (in days) at the beginning. We were all inexperienced and began to overestimate our completion times, as we believed working on a brand new application would be extremely difficult. Over time, our estimates/actual completion times began normalize. We communicated more, completed our retrospectives, and fully realize our capabilities.

Post Mortem

What would you do differently, if you had the chance to start over?
Time management would probably have been the most beneficial to our project. We all have our own priorities: work, other classes, not living in the same city, etc. All of these things were not considered when we began building our project, which eventually led us to starting later than we should have. Our communication was something that came up during our retrospectives as well. We managed to make major improvements with our communication, but if it were strong from beginning, we feel like we could have made some major progress. However, we did manage to produce a fully functional product that we are certainly proud of, aside from all of us having no experience with Android development.

What took the most time? The least? Any Suprises?
A majority of our time was definitely spent on testing. Some parts of our project were built before writing tests, so having to create tests became very time consuming. We were constantly looking over our code, trying to find ways that we could increase our test coverage. We would also have to write tests for our peer’s code implementations, even without fully knowing how it worked. Creating features and implementing them was suprisingly fast for us. We all had great ideas, which we turned into great features/user stories, and in turn we quickly implemented. We played on each other’s strengths and were able to meet most of the goals that we set out to accomplish. After reviewing our velocities, it was very surprising to see how well we all were able to recognize our groups’ capabilities and calculate some very accurate time estimates.
